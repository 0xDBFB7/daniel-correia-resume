Some flagship projects of mine as citations:
[1]: The SafeSump

CEO/CTO of a four-year project to build a failure-resistant sump pump system to prevent basement flooding. Funded by a $37,000 Ontario grant and a $75,000 order.

  • Custom redundant controller with a 120 Mhz SAM4S processor running ~10k lines of high-reliability HITL-simulated OTA-upgradable firmware
  • Custom sub-millivolt ultrasonic and capacitive sensors developed with regard to extreme MTBF and high fouling tolerance
  • A server system capable of handling live updates from several hundred systems, and a scalable data storage system to replicate over 500 Gb realtime over three locations.
  • Dozens of automated programming, testing, calibration and labelling systems
  • Significant vertical integration - lab power supplies, electronic loads[5], pick-and-place machines[6a,b], reflow ovens, thermal cameras [4], paste stencils[3], prototype boards[3], etc were all built in-house (then usually open sourced)
  • Developed almost entirely alone
